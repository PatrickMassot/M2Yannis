Definition 2.3

A complex of \(\mathcal{K}\)-presheaves \(\mathcal{F}^{\bullet }\) is said to be a Homotopy-\(\mathcal{K}\)-sheave if the folowing conditions are satisfied:

  • \begin{equation} \label{axiom:hKsh1} \mathcal{F}^{\bullet }(\varnothing ) \text{ is an acyclic complex} \end{equation}
    1

  • For \(K_1\) and \(K_2\) two comapcts of \(X\) the folowing complex is acyclic:

    \begin{equation} \label{axiom:hKsh2} [\mathcal{F}^{\bullet }(K_1\cup K_2)\to \mathcal{F}^{\bullet -1}(K_1)\bigoplus \mathcal{F}^{\bullet -1}(K_2)\to \mathcal{F}^{\bullet -2}(K_1\cap K_2) ] \end{equation}
    2

  • For any compact \(K\) of \(X\), the following natural morphism is a quasi-isomorphism

    \begin{equation} \label{axiom:hKsh3} \varinjlim \limits _{K\subset U\text{ open relatively compact}}\mathcal{F}^{\bullet }(\overline{U})\to \mathcal{F}^{\bullet }(K) \end{equation}
    3
